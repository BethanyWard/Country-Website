Illyrian Society

Illyrians were divided into tribes that were each their own self-governing community. The most well known
(and also the last) tribe was ruled by King Agron in 3rd century BCE. King Agron died suddenly and left
his widow, Teuta, as the ruling queen. She attacked Sicily and Rome, which led to their conquering and
addition to the roman empire, where they became an important link for trade around Eurpoe. Specificaly,
they traded in wine, oil, cheese, and fish.
